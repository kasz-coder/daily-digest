## PODCASTS

**AI & I by Every: "The AI Sandwich: Where Humans Excel in an AI World"**

Humans are the bread in the sandwich, and the AI is in the middle. That's the core metaphor from Kieran Klaassen, GM of Quora and creator of compound engineering, in a sharp conversation with Every CEO Dan Shipper. The idea: AI has largely solved the execution layer — writing, testing, iterating — but humans remain indispensable at both ends of the process. At the start, to set the frame and define what success looks like. At the end, to judge whether the output feels right.

Kieran's compound engineering framework breaks agentic coding into four steps: plan, work, review, and compound. That last step is the differentiator — where learnings feed back into the repo so agents don't repeat mistakes. One engineer operating this way can ship like a team of five. The "sandwich" framing also explains why full automation keeps moving the goalposts: AI can execute any frame it already understands, but it struggles to generate new frames or to judge subjective quality. That's why Kieran's advice for thriving in an AI-accelerated world is deceptively simple: lean into what brings you joy. The bar for everything else will only rise.
