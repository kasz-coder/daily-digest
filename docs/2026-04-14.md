**Box CEO Aaron Levie** spent another week on the road meeting with enterprise IT and AI leaders across banking, media, retail, healthcare, consulting, tech, and sports. His key takeaways:

- **The shift to agents**: Enterprises are moving from the "chat era" of AI to agents that use tools, process data, and execute real work—moving beyond "let a thousand flowers bloom" to targeted automation.
- **Change management remains huge**: Most workflows aren't ready for agents, and enterprises need help driving these efforts. One company has a head of AI in every business unit reporting to a central team.
- **Token budgeting is a real constraint**: Companies with strict OpEx budgets are debating how to ration compute. Some are trying "shark tank" style pitch sessions for compute budget.
- **Legacy systems are a huge barrier**: Most enterprises have decades-old on-prem or cloud systems that haven't been modernized, preventing agents from tapping data sources unifiedly.
- **Jobs aren't being replaced**: Major agent use cases are automating work the company couldn't do before or couldn't prioritize—software upgrades, back-office processes.

[https://x.com/levie/status/2043426157367095397](https://x.com/levie/status/2043426157367095397)

He also drew a parallel with security, predicting a Jevons paradox: better AI security tooling will *increase* demand for security talent. AI generates 100x more code, leading to more security discoveries. AI can triage threats, but experts are still needed for triage, remediation, and architectural decisions.  
[https://x.com/levie/status/2043318118169354302](https://x.com/levie/status/2043318118169354302)

## Official Blogs

**Claude Blog: Preparing your security program for AI-accelerated offense**

Anthropic announced Project Glasswing—their attempt to apply frontier model capabilities for defensive purposes. Key insights:

- AI models are rapidly reducing the resources, time, and skill required to find and exploit vulnerabilities
- Within 24 months, vast numbers of bugs that sat unnoticed for years will be found by AI models and chained into working exploits
- The good news: defenders can use AI too
- **Recommendations**: Close your patch gap (AI is very effective at recognizing patched vulnerability signatures in unpatched systems), prioritize your software supply chain, expand your scope beyond known vulnerabilities, invest in AI-specific red teaming, and create an AI security incident response plan

[https://claude.com/blog/preparing-your-security-program-for-ai-accelerated-offense](https://claude.com/blog/preparing-your-security-program-for-ai-accelerated-offense)

## Podcasts

**Latent Space: Marc Andreessen introspects on The Death of the Browser, Pi + OpenClaw, and Why "This Time Is Different"**

**The Takeaway**: Andreessen believes we're in an "eighty-year overnight success" moment—AI is drawing on decades of accumulated research that finally unlocked, and the browser as we know it may be dying as AI-native computing interfaces emerge.

The legendary venture capitalist sits down for a wide-ranging conversation covering:

- **Why this time IS different**: The neural network is now confirmed as the correct architecture after 60-70 years of controversy. If Andreessen were 18, he'd spend all his time on this—"this is such an incredible conceptual breakthrough."

- **The death of the browser**: The browser was Microsoft's moat—and now the OS itself is becoming obsolete. As AI-native interfaces emerge, the traditional browser model gets disrupted the same way mobile did to desktop. Pi (his personal AI companion) and OpenClaw represent the new paradigm.

- **The "agentic" shift in enterprise software**: Aaron Levie's enterprise observations (from the X section above) confirm what's happening at ground level—the move from "chat with your data" to "let AI do the work."

- **Security's Jevons paradox**: Just as Andreessen predicted that better AI tooling would increase demand for security talent (not decrease it), the same applies across engineering: AI generates 100x more code, which means more security discoveries, which means more need for human judgment on the other side.

The full episode is available at:  
[https://www.youtube.com/@LatentSpacePod](https://www.youtube.com/@LatentSpacePod)
