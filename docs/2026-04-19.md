**No Priors — "Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott"**

ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott has a contrarian take on the "SaaSpocalypse" narrative: replacing enterprise platforms is extraordinarily expensive, and that cost, combined with the human capital required plus the cost of GPU compute and tokens, makes it 10X more costly to replicate a simple application on their platform with a language model. "People that run businesses understand that people make mistakes. They never will forgive software for making a mistake."

McDermott's leadership philosophy traces back to buying a deli at 16 years old, where he learned that entrepreneurship isn't about having all the answers — it's about being willing to learn and adapt. This shaped his approach to scaling ServiceNow into a $70B+ enterprise: focus on being the platform of platforms, not just another point solution.

On AI adoption in enterprises, McDermott sees a fundamental shift: the winners will be those who build the intelligence layer on top of existing systems rather than replacing them. ServiceNow's strategy is to embed AI into the workflow where work already happens, rather than asking users to switch contexts.
