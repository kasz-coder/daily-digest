**No Priors** — "The Agentic Economy: How AI Agents Will Transform the Financial System with Circle Co-Founder and CEO Jeremy Allaire"

*The Takeaway:* Circle's Jeremy Allaire argues that AI agents will need their own financial infrastructure—programmable money that can handle billions of micro-transactions between autonomous agents—and that blockchain technology is finally ready to deliver it.

Jeremy Allaire, co-founder and CEO of Circle, has been building for thirteen years on the vision of creating "a protocol for dollars on the internet." But his latest thesis goes far beyond stablecoins. In this conversation, he makes the case that AI agents will soon need to pay each other for computation, leading to an "agentic economy" that requires a fundamentally different financial layer.

"We're going through a pretty steep curve right now. We're like in the, you know, about three months into a pretty dramatic shift in kind of the fundamental capabilities of technology, probably the most dramatic that I've ever seen in my own time in technology."

Allaire envisions a world where AI agents consume specialized intelligence from each other, paying fractional amounts in real-time—something traditional banking infrastructure simply cannot support. "We don't have an infrastructure that can work globally, interoperably, instantly, that can be programmed through software layers by arbitrary pieces of software. That doesn't exist."

His solution is ARC, which he describes as an "economic operating system"—designed for the moment when machines play a larger role in economic output. Unlike early crypto networks built for speculation, ARC is built with real money (USDC as the default token), known validator sets from major financial institutions, and privacy primitives from day one.

"We're going to have simultaneously all around the world a renegotiation of the social contract. And it's going require new systems of participation in economics and governance that we haven't had."

On AI's economic impact, Allaire is bullish: "It does feel like we have the potential for double digit GDP numbers in the 2030s."
