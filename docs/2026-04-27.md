## PODCASTS

**No Priors: Scaling Global Organizations in the Age of AI with ServiceNow CEO Bill McDermott**

The Takeaway: Replacing an enterprise SaaS platform in the age of AI is so expensive and disruptive that it makes the vaunted "SaaS apocalypse" look like a fantasy — but the real leverage comes from AI augmenting human capital on top of existing platforms, not replacing them.

Bill McDermott, the closest thing enterprise tech has to a rock star, came up the hard way — he bought a deli at 16, stocked shelves, pumped gas, and bussed tables before climbing to CEO of SAP and now ServiceNow. His philosophy on leadership is rooted in that scrappiness: he hires hungry people, not polished ones.

On the "SaaS apocalypse" theory — the idea that AI would blow up existing software and rebuild from scratch — McDermott is blunt. The math doesn't work. Replacing an enterprise platform means the cost of migration, plus the cost of GPU compute and tokens, plus the human capital doing the migration work instead of actual business. For a simple application on ServiceNow's platform, replicating it with a language model costs 10x more. Enterprise customers don't want to rip and replace; they want AI on top of what they already have.

His most quotable line cuts through the AI hype cycle: "People that run businesses understand that people make mistakes. They never will forgive software for making a mistake." The standard for AI in the enterprise is unforgiving — and that reality hasn't changed just because the models got better.
