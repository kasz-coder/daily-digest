**Unsupervised Learning** — OpenAI Chief Scientist on Continual Learning, RL Beyond Code, and Future Alignment

The Takeaway: OpenAI's chief scientist sees continual learning as the key unsolved problem in AI, with longer-term alignment challenges centering on how models generalize values across unseen situations.

Ajak Choi, arguably one of the most important people in AI today, joined Unsupervised Learning to discuss what's required to make long-running agents work, OpenAI's progress in AI for science, and how companies should think about reinforcement learning and the evolution of harnesses.

A key insight: the longer-term challenge with alignment is about generalization — what values does the model fall back on when encountering situations it hasn't been trained on? The team is focused on building models that can learn continuously rather than just from static datasets.

They also discussed OpenAI's work on math competitions and what it means for model reasoning capabilities. The research organization is in a "focusing moment" as they think about how to run teams effectively while pushing the frontier forward.
