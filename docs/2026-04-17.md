**Acquired** — "The AI Race: Who's Winning?"

In this episode, the hosts analyze the current state of the AI race between major players. The key insight: the competitive landscape has shifted from model performance to ecosystem lock-in, with every major company racing to build developer moats through integrated tooling, infrastructure, and platform services.

**The Takeaway:** The AI race is no longer just about who has the best model — it's about who can build the most defensible ecosystem. Companies that control the full stack from infrastructure to application layer are positioning themselves for long-term dominance.

Key points from the conversation:

- **Model commoditization:** As base models become more similar, differentiation is moving up the stack to developer experience, tooling, and vertical integration.

- **Enterprise adoption patterns:** The "forward-deployed engineer" model that Aaron Levie discussed is becoming the standard way AI gets sold into enterprises — not just API calls but comprehensive workflow solutions.

- **Open source dynamics:** The tension between open-source models and proprietary ones is intensifying, with some arguing the "bitter lesson" will eventually favor open weights, while others point to the enormous compute requirements as a barrier.

- **Agentic workflow shift:** The conversation emphasized that we're in the early innings of the agentic paradigm, where the real value isn't in the models themselves but in how they're composed into autonomous workflows.

- **Geographic competition:** Beyond US companies, Chinese AI progress is being watched closely, with Peter Yang's observations from his China trip highlighting the intensity of development there.
