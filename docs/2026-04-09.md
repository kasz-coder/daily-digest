**Latent Space — "Extreme Harness Engineering for Token Billionaires: 1M LOC, 1B toks/day, 0% human code, 0% human review — Ryan Lopopolo, OpenAI Frontier & Symphony"**

*The Takeaway: The most effective AI engineering teams are building "harness systems" that let AI agents autonomously write, execute, test, and review millions of lines of code with zero human intervention—and this is becoming the new moat in AI product development.*

Ryan Lopopolo works on frontier product exploration at OpenAI, building new products on the enterprise platform. He wrote the defining article on "harness engineering"—a discipline that emerged from the observation that AI coding agents need sophisticated scaffolding to operate at scale. The key insight: you need to take a systems thinking mindset and constantly ask where the agent is making mistakes, where you're spending your time, and how to automate that time away.

The conversation gets specific about what "extreme" harness engineering looks like in practice: systems handling one million lines of code and one billion tokens per day, with zero human-written code and zero human review. This isn't theoretical—it's happening at frontier AI labs today. The harness becomes a core part of building AI products, especially as models get better at coding. The natural progression is collapsing a user journey you're trying to solve into code and using the Codex harness to solve it for you.

What makes this a genuine discipline rather than just good engineering is the pattern recognition: when you can automate away the SDLC (software development lifecycle) entirely, you unlock a fundamentally different velocity. The harness does all the wiring and lets you communicate in prompts to let the model cook—but the sophisticated part is knowing where to intervene and build confidence in the automation. "You have to step back and constantly be asking where is the agent making mistakes."
