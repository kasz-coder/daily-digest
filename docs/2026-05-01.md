**Aditya Agarwal (adityaag on X)** — General Partner at South Park Commons, Ex-CTO at Dropbox
After spending a lot of time installing, using, debugging and building with OpenClaw and other agents, shared some candid observations:

- Agents are developer products today. They're far from consumer grade in terms of delight, simplicity, and reliability.
- Agents generate chat interfaces, but he wishes they generated more dynamic interfaces — interactive apps and webpages instead.
- The agents are ultimately a wrapper to code-generation and tool-calling, not "generative AI." This is important to grok.

The Takeaway: Stripe sees AI companies hitting $30M ARR in 18 months — 3x faster than the 2018 SaaS class — and AI agents are becoming the new predominant actors on the Internet, buying and selling from each other.

Emily Glassberg Sands leads data and AI at Stripe, which sees 2% of global GDP. She's seeing a fundamental shift: the Internet economy is becoming more autonomous. Machines are becoming users of developer infrastructure. LLM traffic to Stripe Docs is up 10x year over year.

On trust: Most people still don't trust AI with larger online purchases. People are hesitant to let AI make expensive purchases like a vacation or a couch — just like the early days of online shopping. But a superhero outfit for a kid who needs one? Sure, let the agent handle it.

On fraud: It used to mean stolen credit cards. Now attackers are stealing free-trial tokens and compute credits. Free-trial abuse has 4x-ed in the last six months. AI is on both sides — fraudsters are using it to scale attacks, while Stripe is using it to detect them. They're blocking 250,000 fraudulent free trials a week for one large customer.

On growth: AI companies are growing faster than any cohort Stripe has ever tracked. Top companies hit $30M ARR in 18 months — 3x faster than the 2018 SaaS class. So far, it's net new spend instead of cannibalized software budgets.

On pricing: Outcome-based billing is replacing seat-based pricing.
